# Shared Drafting Principles & Assumptions

**Last Updated:** April 20, 2026

## Purpose

This document records common drafting assumptions used across Outer-Void ecosystem legal and trust documentation.

## Practical Nature of These Documents

These documents are operational, public-facing policies meant to communicate expected behavior, data practices, and risk boundaries in plain language. They are not legal advice.

## Evolving Implementation Assumption

Product behavior may evolve over time. Features, integrations, account models, and moderation systems can change. Policies are written to remain broadly accurate while avoiding unsupported technical claims.

## Specific-over-General Rule

Product-level documents govern the behavior of a specific product. Outer-Void umbrella policies govern general ecosystem properties. Shared baseline documents provide interpretation support where product docs are silent.

## Conservative Claim Standard

Documentation should avoid:
- Unsupported compliance claims
- Unsupported security guarantees
- Unsupported regulatory status claims
- Invented corporate or jurisdiction details

## Reliability and Limits

Policies describe expected operations, not perfect outcomes. No policy text should be read as a guarantee of uninterrupted service, complete detection accuracy, or legal enforceability in all jurisdictions.
