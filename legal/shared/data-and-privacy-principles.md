# Shared Data & Privacy Principles

**Last Updated:** April 20, 2026

## Privacy Posture

Outer-Void ecosystem products are designed to collect and use data only as reasonably needed to operate services, maintain security, communicate with users, and improve reliability.

## Typical Categories of Data

Depending on the product, data may include:
- Account and identity data (such as usernames, profile identifiers, and contact details users choose to provide)
- Usage and interaction data (such as feature usage, submissions, and activity logs)
- Device/network metadata (such as approximate technical diagnostics used for security and troubleshooting)
- Communications data (such as support requests or feedback)
- Transaction-related metadata for paid features, where relevant

## Typical Uses of Data

Data may be used to:
- Deliver features and maintain accounts
- Moderate abuse, fraud, and misuse
- Improve product quality and service stability
- Provide support and service communications
- Meet legal obligations and protect platform safety

## Service Providers and Infrastructure

Products may rely on hosting, analytics, messaging, communications, payment, infrastructure, or security providers. These providers may process data on behalf of the service within their own contractual and policy frameworks.

## Security and Limits

Reasonable technical and organizational safeguards may be used, but no internet service is completely secure. Policies do not guarantee absolute prevention of unauthorized access.

## User Rights and Requests

Users may request access, correction, deletion, or clarification through the contact methods shown on the relevant product site or interface. Some requests may be limited by legal obligations, technical constraints, fraud prevention, and platform safety requirements.
